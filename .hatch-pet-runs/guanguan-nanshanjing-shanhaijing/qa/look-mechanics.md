# Guanguan look mechanics

Keep both feet and the rounded owl torso planted. The eyes lead within their natural sockets, followed by a restrained head yaw/pitch; the brow, beak, crest curl, and shoulder feathers follow continuously. Wings stay folded and body scale stays fixed.

- 000 up: eyes and beak angle up; eyelids open slightly; crest rises.
- 090 screen-right: beak tip and pupils cross to screen-right; left facial plane and left shoulder are more visible.
- 180 down: gaze and beak tip lower; brow softens; head tucks slightly.
- 270 screen-left: beak tip and pupils cross to screen-left; right facial plane and right shoulder are more visible.

Interpolate evenly with no body spin, wing gesture, prop, shadow, or googly-eye replacement.
