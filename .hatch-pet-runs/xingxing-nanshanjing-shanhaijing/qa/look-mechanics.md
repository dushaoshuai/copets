# Xingxing look mechanics

Keep hands/feet and crouched lower body registered. The eyes lead, then the muzzle and head turn or pitch; ears and mane follow, shoulders shift subtly, and the long curled tail lags continuously while staying attached. Preserve the brown fur, pale ears/mane, facial proportions, and primate anatomy.

- 000 up: eyes and face pitch up; brow and eyelids participate; neck extends slightly.
- 090 screen-right: pupils, nose, and muzzle turn right; left cheek/ear becomes more visible; tail lags left.
- 180 down: gaze and muzzle lower; chin tucks; shoulders compress slightly.
- 270 screen-left: pupils, nose, and muzzle turn left; right cheek/ear becomes more visible; tail lags right.

Avoid broad raster warps, skull stretching, static eyes, whole-body rotation, or tail jumps.
